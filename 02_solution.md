# The Solution
### Studex — Working Document

---

## What Studex Does

Studex is a mobile app that answers one question every Indian college student asks every morning: *what do I have today?*

That's the core. Everything else is built around that daily habit.

Three features. Each one targets a specific daily pain point. No feature exists because it looks good in a demo — each one exists because I personally ran into the problem it solves.

---

## Feature 01 — Today View (Smart Schedule)

The home screen shows today's classes. Clean list. Times. Venue if known. That's it.

The timetable is set up once during onboarding. After that it just works. No scrolling through WhatsApp. No asking someone. No guessing.

Class cancelled? The CR can push an update. It shows up instantly. The student knows before they've left the house.

This is the reason a student opens the app every day. Daily habit = the foundation everything else sits on.

---

## Feature 02 — Subject Folders (Files)

Every subject gets a folder. PDFs, notes, links, past papers — organized by subject, searchable, permanent.

Currently this all lives in WhatsApp and gets buried within hours. Studex gives it a home that doesn't move.

The CR can push files directly to the right folder. Students can share within their section. Everything stays findable.

This is the feature students use hardest around exams — and the one that keeps them coming back, because once your files are in Studex, you have to come back to get them.

---

## Feature 03 — Bunk Tracker (Attendance)

Students log attendance themselves — attended or not. The app tracks their percentage against the 75% requirement in real time.

It tells them: how many classes they can still miss. How many they need to attend to recover. Whether today's bunk is safe or risky.

No dependence on the college portal. No lag. No broken mobile interface. The student controls their own data and knows exactly where they stand.

This removes the anxiety that's otherwise just background noise for most of college.

---

## What It's Not

Not a college portal replacement. Not an LMS. Not a learning platform.

It doesn't need the college to cooperate. It works regardless of how good or bad a college's infrastructure is. The student is the user, the student is the customer, and everything is built around that.

---

## Day One Experience

Student downloads the app. Signs up with student email. Answers a few basics — college, course, year, section. Picks which feature they want first. Gets a short setup guide. Done.

First thing most students go to: today's schedule. They get an answer immediately. That's the hook.

No empty state. No "invite your friends to get started." Useful on day one, alone, with no one else on the platform.

---

## How It Grows Over Time

**Right now (MVP):** Schedule, files, attendance. The three daily pain points. Fast to build, fast to validate.

**Next:** Section-level sharing. CR announcement broadcasts. Peer notes exchange. These make it stickier and turn CRs into active advocates.

**Later:** Exam prep layer — syllabus tracking, past papers, study reminders. The features most valuable in the 3 weeks before exams, layered on top of a daily habit already built.

**Eventually:** Colleges that want to plug in officially can pay for institutional access. But this is never required for the student product to work.

---

## The One Design Rule

Every feature passes one test: does this make the student's day less chaotic?

Not: does this look impressive. Not: does this give the institution control. Not: does this add features for the sake of features.

Less chaotic. That's the only test.

---

## Where We Are Right Now

The product is currently on paper. MVP can be built in approximately one week using AI-assisted development tools. The goal is a working, presentable prototype within that window — something real people can open on their phones and actually use.

We're not waiting for the perfect build. We're building the minimum version that answers the question: *do students actually use this every day?* Everything after that depends on the answer.
